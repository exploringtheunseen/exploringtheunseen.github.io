# Hello, World!

We're a team of curious characters interested in developing our data visualisation and analysis chops. The best way
to learn is by doing, and we have set up this project to serve as a platform and repository for our experiments.
The current pandemic is a tragedy, but it is also unique in the amount of data that is being 
collected and desseminated. In fact, the amount of data is so vast, that it is sometimes tough to make sense of it all. 
Our goal is to focus on the Indian picture. There are brilliant resources that are reporting on the spread in different states, 
both government and non-government (https://twitter.com/ShamikaRavi, https://github.com/devdatalab/covid 
and https://www.covid19india.org/). We hope to stand on the footsteps of these giants and through our trials, discover new
insights, dependencies and perspectives to develop a deeper undertanding of the world around us.

Broadly speaking, we aim to improve our data visualization capabilities from a *technology perspective*:
- Go beyond line charts and bar graphs! 
- Understand best practices and new approaches in the field of data visualization
- Integrate data from different sources automatically
- Develop workflows to integrate new data as it becomes available
- Develop tools so that users (thats you!) can select what relations you would like to look at, and the best way of doing so.

from an *analysis perspective*:
- Identify visualizations for different scenarios so that connections between variables are obvious
- ... or anomalies. or outliers...
- Guard against our own biases!
- Develop visualizations that pose questions and provoke debate 

and from a *Indian/Covid-19 perspective*:
- What is the impact of different socio-economic factors on the spread of Corona virus?
- In what way are some states/districts/regions doing better than others? What are the underlying reasons?
- What is the 'State of the Nation,' based purely on data?

We invite you to contribute your thoughts, visualizations and analyses! We hope that you will learn with us, and that we 
will learn from you, in turn.
